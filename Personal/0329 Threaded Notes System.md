# Introduction
## What is this about?
An ontology and conceptual design about managing one's life, followed by implementation instructions for an AI agent using Obsidian notes.

## What makes a life management system good?
1) Robustness to cycles and adherence levels
2) Ergonomics that scale from simple and unstructured to complex and structured
3) Measurable progress
4) Capability to balance and prioritize between life's domains

## What this is not about?
We won't tackle here higher level life planning like visions, goals, key results, etc. This is a more to-do centric approach, that can scale from simple atomic actions to complex programs

# Concepts

## Thread
A thread is anything that should be registered and will eventually entail some sort of action or actions, from list of books to read, business ideas, routines, outstanding task and entire businesses (at least from a personal viewpoint). The name is inspired from computer threads and forum/conversational threads.

A thread **might have** **thread type**, which is not a central element to the ontology, is just a way to quickly access what the thread is about. These types may change over time, but here is the initial list:
- **evaluate**: when specific type wasn't assigned to a thread or it was just created for later processing
- **list**: a list of related things (e.g. books to read, money lent) that requires no particular action - the purpose is strictly registering.
- **task**: a simple activity which is **usually** atomic, simple and can be done in a day, and eventually is closed or comes to an end.
- **mission**: a complex task which **usually** is multi-step, takes more than a day and less than a month and it might require requiring research and planning between steps; can be composed of subtasks; comes to a defined end.
- **project**: a complex set of activities than take more than a month, might require iterations of research and planning, but comes to a defined end; it can be composed of tasks, missions and lists.
- program: the highest complexity thread which has no defined end (e.g., become a master in AI), but might have milestones, goals, key results (tbc)
