## What This Is

The Codex is the stable, slow-changing layer of André's identity. Not goals (that's Vision.md). Not plans (that's Life Plan 2026). The things that would still be true in 10 years.

It exists so that agents — especially Apollo and eventually the André clone — can understand not just what André wants to do, but how he thinks, what he values, and what makes him him.

**It grows slowly.** Apollo adds to it when patterns are observed, when André articulates something important, when a conversation reveals something worth preserving. It is never written all at once.

## Identity & Character

- **Carioca, born 1988, Tijuca, Rio de Janeiro.** Lived in São Paulo ages 1–10 before returning to Rio after his parents' divorce. Both parents now in Rio.
- **Raw intelligence, pattern-first thinker.** Not a memorizer — a pattern extractor. The childhood story is the clearest early example: when faced with 20 items to memorize, he found the structural rule that reduced them to 4. This is not a trick; it's how he processes everything. He finds the underlying model before engaging with the surface.
- **Autodidact under pressure.** Learned to learn early, often unconventionally. Slept through classes, caught up independently. Performed at the top while appearing not to try.
- **Competitive but indifferent to the rules of competition.** Won physics competitions, then showed up to the state championship sunburnt, ill, with a stolen pen and no eraser, wrote with blood and water dripping on the paper — and got everything right. Was disqualified. The correct answers mattered more to him than the medal.
- **Self-aware about his cycles.** Diligence → drift → reflection → rebuild. Recognizes this pattern fully and designs around it rather than fighting it.
- **Generalist with deep spikes.** End-to-end delivery is his natural mode. Pure specialization bores him. He is very good at many things and the best at none — and he's made peace with that as a feature, not a bug.

## Operating Patterns

- **Top-down thinker.** Needs to see the big picture before descending into details. Can't operate comfortably from a list of tasks without understanding the strategy behind them.
- **Extraordinarily focused when there's one clear thing.** The challenge is always prioritization, not capability. When context narrows, he executes at a high level.
- **Loses faith in systems when they get complex.** The system needs to survive his off-weeks, not just his on-weeks.
- **Under-estimates time on things he finds interesting.** The agent system took longer than expected because it engaged him. This is a consistent pattern.
- **Works late, historically nocturnal.** The 4am session writing this system is not unusual.
- **ADHD characteristics.** On Venvanse 70mg. Effect reportedly becoming harder to notice — exploring new brand which worked better (noted March 2026).

## What He Actually Cares About

**In roughly this order, based on what he wrote and how he wrote it:**

1. **Enzo** — his 11-year-old half-brother from his father's side. The only person he says he truly cares about. Enzo's unconditional greeting when André came home drunk and broken in his darker years is the memory he keeps. "I'm not sure if I would still exist if he wasn't around." This is the load-bearing emotional fact of his life.
2. **Women and sexuality.** He names this plainly and without apology. Long-term: high-quality relationships or roster. A priority because he is getting older and his standards are not decreasing.
3. **Wealth and financial freedom.** Not for its own sake — to make the people he cares about happy, and to fund the life he wants. He understands money doesn't bring happiness and pursues it anyway.
4. **Mastery and reputation in AI/ML.** Wants to be recognized as an authority, not just a practitioner.
5. **Freedom and experiences.** Travel, nature, activities. These recharge him but get chronically deferred.
