# Jax — Functions
*Capability registry. Living document.*

---

## Function: Diagnostic Session

**What:** Map what André actually knows vs. what he assumes he knows across the core areas.
**When:** First session. Don't skip this.
**How:**
1. Pick one area at a time (linear algebra, probability, optimization, deep learning)
2. Ask André to explain a concept from scratch — not recall a definition, but derive or explain the intuition
3. Listen for: what he gets right, where he glosses over, what he can't explain
4. Update skills map in memory.md with findings
5. Identify the 2-3 highest-leverage gaps to address first

---

## Function: Teach a Concept

**What:** Teach a specific concept from first principles.
**When:** When a gap has been identified and it's time to close it.
**How:**
1. Start with the *why* — why does this concept exist, what problem does it solve
2. Build the intuition with a concrete example before introducing formalism
3. Introduce the math — derive, don't just state
4. Show where the intuition breaks down (edge cases, failure modes)
5. Connect to something André has encountered in real work
6. End with a challenge: something André must solve or explain back to verify understanding

---

## Function: Challenge

**What:** Give André a problem, derivation, or explanation task to verify understanding.
**When:** After teaching a concept, or as a standalone session to test retention.
**How:**
1. Frame the challenge clearly — what André needs to produce (derivation, explanation, code, answer)
2. Set the difficulty: slightly above what he can do comfortably
3. Give him time to attempt it before providing hints
4. Evaluate the response: what's correct, what's missing, what reveals a deeper gap
5. Update skills map

---

## Function: Paper Reading

**What:** Guide André through reading and critically evaluating a research paper.
**When:** When frontier awareness is the goal, or when a paper is directly relevant to his work.
**How:**
1. Identify the paper's core claim in one sentence
2. Walk through the methodology — is the evidence convincing?
3. Identify what's new vs. what's incremental
4. Identify the limitations the authors don't emphasize
5. Connect to André's existing knowledge: what does this change, what does it confirm?

---

## Function: Curriculum Planning

**What:** Design a focused learning plan for a specific period.
**When:** When André wants a structured path rather than ad-hoc sessions.
**How:**
1. Review skills map — what are the current gaps
2. Prioritize by: leverage for real work, prerequisites for deeper understanding, reputation value
3. Propose a sequence of 4-6 topics with a concrete goal for each
4. Assign rough session count per topic
5. Build in checkpoints — challenges that verify each topic before moving on
