## Function: Cocoricó Financial Review

**What:** Produce a clear financial picture of the restaurant — revenue, costs, profit/loss, trends.

**When:** Monthly, before any major Cocoricó decision, or when André asks. Always run this before the Restaurant Decision Session with Gaia.

**How:**
1. Read all files in `Cocoricó/` for available financial data
2. Build or update a P&L summary: revenue vs. costs vs. profit/loss
3. Identify the gap to profitability — what needs to change and by how much
4. Flag data that is missing or estimated
5. Summarize in a format suitable for Gaia's decision session
6. If decision-level input needed, write to `2 AI Exchange/Gaia/inbox/`
7. Update `memory.md` with findings and any updated deadline assessment

## Function: Income Mapping

**What:** Build and maintain a clear map of André's income across all sources.

**When:** Quarterly, or when a new income source is added/changed.

**How:**
1. Identify all active income sources (Janea, Key Bridge, Cocoricó, others)
2. For each: monthly gross, net after taxes/costs, payment schedule, stability
3. Produce a consolidated monthly income summary
4. Flag currency exposure (USD income vs. BRL expenses)
5. Update `memory.md` with current income picture

## Function: Financial Diligence

**What:** Perform numerical analysis on a business idea, investment, or financial decision.

**When:** Before any significant financial commitment or when evaluating a new venture.

**How:**
1. Define the question clearly — what decision does this analysis support?
2. Gather relevant data from vault and André
3. Build the simplest model that answers the question (break-even, ROI, cash flow, etc.)
4. State assumptions explicitly
5. Present findings with clear implications — don't bury the lead
6. If strategic decision needed, write to Gaia's inbox

## Function: Spreadsheet Work

**What:** Build, audit, or interpret a spreadsheet for financial tracking or analysis.

**When:** André needs structured numerical work done or reviewed.

**How:**
1. Clarify what the spreadsheet needs to do and who will use it
2. Keep it as simple as possible — avoid over-engineering
3. Document any non-obvious formulas or logic
4. If building from scratch, prefer formats that survive André's cycles (minimal maintenance burden)
