## Function: Build P&L

**What:** Construct a Profit & Loss statement from available data.

**When:** First priority — needs to happen before any meaningful decision can be made.

**How:**
1. Ask André for: monthly revenue (by platform), COGS (chicken, ingredients, packaging), labor costs, platform fees (iFood %), delivery costs, overhead (rent, utilities, gas), marketing spend, any other recurring costs
2. Build a table: Revenue - COGS = Gross Profit - Labor - Overhead - Platform Fees - Other = Net Profit/Loss
3. Note data quality issues clearly (estimated vs known)
4. Calculate gross margin and net margin
5. Save to `Cocoricó/PL_[month].md`

## Function: Break-Even Analysis

**What:** Calculate what revenue level is needed to break even.

**When:** Once P&L baseline exists.

**How:**
1. Identify fixed costs (rent, base labor, subscriptions)
2. Identify variable costs and their % of revenue (COGS, platform fees, packaging)
3. Calculate: Break-even = Fixed Costs / (1 - Variable Cost %)
4. Compare to current average revenue
5. Identify the gap and what levers could close it

## Function: Decision Framework — Stay vs Exit

**What:** Structure the June/July 2026 decision with full financial clarity.

**When:** Once P&L and break-even are known; at least once before June.

**How:**
1. **Stay scenario:** What would need to be true? Revenue increase needed, cost cuts available, partner involvement, timeline to profitability. Is it realistic?
2. **Exit scenario:** What are the costs? Equipment (owned vs leased), deposits, outstanding payments, platform contracts, team notice. What does André get back?
3. **Opportunity cost:** What does André do with ~10-15h/week and the mental bandwidth currently spent on Cocoricó?
4. Present as a clear comparison table
5. Do not make the decision for André — present the picture and ask what he sees

## Function: Scenario Modeling

**What:** Model financial impact of a specific change (price increase, cost cut, new partner, platform change).

**When:** When André is considering a specific operational or strategic change.

**How:**
1. Clarify what change is being modeled and its assumptions
2. Take baseline P&L
3. Apply the change and show new P&L
4. Calculate impact on net profit and break-even
5. Note what would need to be true for the scenario to work
