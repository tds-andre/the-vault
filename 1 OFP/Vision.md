Not a role or a title. A way of being.

- **A builder** — someone who creates businesses, systems and ideas, not just executes for others

## Domains & Where I Want To Be

### 💼 Professional (Janea + Key Bridge)
- Engineering is the engine right now — it funds everything else
- The ceiling is real: I want to progressively shift income from labor to assets
- Short term: be irreplaceable at both jobs, deepen AI/Agents/LLM skills (the gap I know I have)
- Medium term: transition from engineer-for-hire to engineer-who-builds-his-own-things
- **Mastery goal:** Become a recognized authority in my field — not just a good practitioner, but someone known for a point of view. This means publishing, building in public, going to events, and developing a body of work that speaks for itself. The "What You Bring" file is a starting point for understanding what I already have to offer.

### 🍗 Cocoricó
- The goal was always proof-of-concept for investor credibility, not empire-building through restaurants
- Hard checkpoint: **profitable by June/July 2026, or clean exit**
- If it turns: systematize it, delegate it, extract the lesson
- If it doesn't: take the loss consciously, document what I learned, move on without guilt
- The real value was learning to run a business with my own capital — that knowledge doesn't disappear

### 🏗️ Building (Next Business)
- The restaurant model (self-finance → prove → seek investment) doesn't scale fast enough
- Next venture needs external capital from the start, or a fundamentally different model
- I have strong domain knowledge in ML/data, strong end-to-end delivery, and a growing ability to see business problems clearly — that's the asset
- No rush to define the idea now, but keep the antenna up and document what I notice
- Active subthreads in incubation: **MiniMarket**, **X In Rio** — these live in `3 Subthreads/`

### 🤝 Social & Women

## What I Know About Myself

*These are operating truths, not judgments.*

- I'm a generalist with deep spikes — end-to-end is my natural mode, pure specialization bores me
- I can be extraordinarily focused when I have one clear thing — the challenge is always prioritization, not capability
- I run in cycles: diligence → drift → reflection → rebuild. This is my rhythm, not a flaw. Design for it.
- I lose faith in systems when they get complex. Keep everything as simple as possible.
- I work best when I can see the big picture first, then descend into tasks
- I have strong analytical instincts and a genuine ability to frame messy problems clearly — this is rare and valuable

---

## What I'm Not Chasing

*Knowing what you don't want is as useful as knowing what you do.*

- I'm not chasing a title or prestige for its own sake
- I'm not trying to be the world's best ML engineer — I'm trying to build leverage
- I'm not trying to run 5 restaurants — I want assets that compound, not operations that demand
- I'm not trying to be perfect at productivity — I want a system robust enough to survive my own cycles
